About Me

Service Timeline

  1. Jan 2019

    Enlisted – Army National Guard

    Enlisted in the Minnesota Army National Guard during my junior year of high school through the Split Option Program, which allowed me to begin my military service while still completing my education.

    This program gave me a year’s head start on my military career, enabling me to attend Basic Combat Training in the summer of 2019, return to finish my senior year, and then complete my Military Occupational Specialty (MOS) training in the summer of 2020.

    My recruiter SSG Hong and I at MEPS after the swear in ceremony.

    The pic we took reminds me of that scene of Michael Scott lol.

  2. Aug 2019

    Graduated From Basic Combat Training (10 Weeks)

    I attended Basic Combat Training (BCT) with Foxtrot Company, 2nd Battalion – 13th Infantry Regiment, where I spent 10 weeks developing the foundational skills and discipline required to serve as a U.S. Army Soldier.

    During this time, I learned not only the basics of marksmanship, land navigation, first aid, drill and ceremony, and field operations — but also how to lead, follow, and work as part of a cohesive team under pressure.

    I was honored to be recognized as one of the top performers in my company and was given the privilege of carrying the Company Guidon during graduation.

  3. May 2020

    Graduated From High School

    Graduated from Como Park Senior Highschool in Roseville, Minnesota with a 3.0 GPA.

    The following Summer I headed to Fort Lee, Virginia to get trained to become a 91B - Wheeled Vehicle Mechanic.

  4. Sep 2020

    Graduated From Advanced Individual Training (14 Weeks)

    I attended Advanced Individual Training (AIT) at Fort Lee, Virginia for 14 weeks, where I trained to become a 91B Wheeled Vehicle Mechanic.

    This phase of training was focused on building both technical expertise and professional discipline. We learned to diagnose, maintain, and repair tactical vehicles — everything from Humvees to larger transport and recovery trucks — ensuring mission readiness and safety for Soldiers in the field.

    I graduated at the top of my class, earning an Army Achievement Medal (AAM) for maintaining a 100% score on all written and hands-on evaluations.

    Our company’s name was “Avengers” and I was in the 3rd Platoon "Daredevils".

    Fun fact: nearly half of my platoon which was composed of about 30 Soldiers — were from Minnesota

  5. April 2021

    State Active Duty

    I was activated during the trial of former police officer Derek Chauvin following the death of George Floyd, I was deployed to Brooklyn Center, Minnesota as part of a statewide security mission.

    Our primary role was to maintain public safety and help de-escalate tensions between protesters and law enforcement during a highly charged period of civil unrest.

    We were temporarily staged in a firestation before our next post location was decided. Some people took naps until it was time to move out.

  6. Mar 2022

    COVID-19 Nursing Home Mission (3 Months)

    Activated by Governor Tim Walz to support Minnesota’s long-term care facilities during the statewide CNA shortage, I was deployed on a three-month mission to Bemidji and St. Cloud, Minnesota.

    Serving as a Temporary Nursing Assistant, I provided hands-on care and support for vulnerable residents throughout the COVID-19 pandemic.

    One of the most meaningful experiences was using my bilingual skills to communicate with and aid an elderly Vietnamese man voice his wants and needs in the Dementia unit — a moment that highlighted the importance of cultural connection and compassion in service.

  7. Jun 2025

    Graduated From Basic Leaders Course (22 Days)

    Promotion to Sergeant – Fort McCoy, Wisconsin. After 22 intensive days of leadership training from June 5th to June 27th, I graduated 18th out of 131 other Sergeants in both academics and Soldier skills.

    This earned me a place on the Commandant’s List — a prestigious recognition awarded to the top 20% of graduates who demonstrated exceptional performance and leadership potential.
